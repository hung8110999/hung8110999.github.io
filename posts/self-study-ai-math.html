Why I Decided to Self-Study AI and Mathematics

Traditional education is great, but sometimes you need to forge your own path to learn exactly what you want at your own pace. For a long time, I found myself constrained by the rigid structure of standard curriculums. I had a burning curiosity for Artificial Intelligence, but I quickly realized that to truly understand the algorithms and the models powering modern technology, I needed a deep foundation in Mathematics.

The Realization

When I first started looking into Machine Learning, the coding part wasn't the hardest hurdle to overcome. Writing a script to train a basic model using Scikit-Learn or PyTorch was straightforward. The real challenge arose when I asked the question "Why?" Why did this model converge to this specific minimum? Why did changing the loss function completely alter the behavior of the network?

The answer was always hidden behind mathematical concepts: Multivariate Calculus, Linear Algebra, and Probability. Without mastering these, I was just treating AI as a black box.

Taking the Self-Taught Route

I made a definitive choice. I would build my own curriculum. Starting from the absolute basics, I mapped out a learning trajectory for Calculus, moving into Linear Algebra, and bridging that into Advanced Statistics.

  • Calculus: Understanding derivatives and optimizing functions.
  • Linear Algebra: Getting comfortable with high-dimensional spaces, matrices, and eigenvectors.
  • Probability: Handling uncertainty and understanding distributions.

I created this blog to document this exact process. By recording what I learn every single day, I hope to hold myself accountable, organize my thoughts, and perhaps help others who are embarking on a similar path. Learning is not a straight line, but every concept grasped is a new tool in the toolbox. Let's keep building!
