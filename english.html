1 Grammar Essentials

‚ñº

Strong grammar is the backbone of clear communication. Key areas to master:

  • Tenses: Past, Present, Future (Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous)
  • Parts of Speech: Nouns, Verbs, Adjectives, Adverbs, Prepositions
  • Sentence Structure: Subject-Verb-Object, compound & complex sentences
  • Articles & Determiners: a, an, the ‚Äî when and how to use them
  • Conditionals: If clauses (Type 0, 1, 2, 3, and mixed)

Tip: Practice by writing daily journal entries and checking your grammar!

2 Vocabulary Building

‚ñº

A rich vocabulary helps you express ideas precisely and understand what you read.

  • Learn word roots, prefixes, and suffixes
  • Use spaced repetition (flashcards, Anki)
  • Read extensively ‚Äî books, articles, news
  • Learn words in context, not in isolation
  • Keep a vocabulary journal with example sentences

Daily Goal: Learn 5-10 new words per day with their usage.

3 Reading Comprehension

‚ñº

Reading is the best way to improve your overall English ability.

  • Start with graded readers at your level
  • Practice skimming (main idea) and scanning (specific info)
  • Summarize each paragraph in your own words
  • Look up unknown words, note them, and review
  • Read diverse genres: news, fiction, academic, blogs

4 Writing Skills

‚ñº

Writing helps you organize your thoughts and is essential for academic and professional life.

  • Paragraph structure: Topic sentence ‚Üí Supporting details ‚Üí Conclusion
  • Essay types: Argumentative, Descriptive, Narrative, Expository
  • Connectors: However, Therefore, In addition, On the other hand
  • Editing: Always proofread for grammar, clarity, and flow
  • Practice: Write every day, even if it's just a few sentences

5 Listening & Speaking

‚ñº

Communication is king. Here are strategies to improve your speaking and listening:

  • Watch English content with subtitles (movies, YouTube, podcasts)
  • Shadow native speakers ‚Äî repeat what they say
  • Record yourself and compare with native pronunciation
  • Practice conversation with language exchange partners
  • Focus on pronunciation, intonation, and rhythm
